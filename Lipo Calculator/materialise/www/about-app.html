Lipocalculator Format*

For the Fat Aspirate:

Surgeon and Facility Experience
Full value for experience over 1000 cases
Reduced values by 20% for 100-999 cases
Reduced values by 40% for under 100 cases
Age of Patient
        Full value for ages 18-59
        Reduced values by 5% for ages 60-74
        Reduced values by 10% for ages above 75
Number of Liposuction Areas
        Full values for 1-7 areas
        Reduced values by 10% for over 7 areas
Estimate Liposuction Fat Aspirate
        Full values for up to 5 liters
        Reduced values for over 5 liters
Combined Procedures
        Full values for breast or facial surgery
        Reduced values by 10% for abdominoplasty
        Reduced values by 20% for body or thigh lift
BMI greater than 30, Reduce value by 10%
History of MI, Reduce value by 50%
History of CVA, Reduce value by 50%
History of DVT, PE, or Family History of Clotting, Reduce value by 10%
History of 3 or more lost pregnancies, Reduce value by 10%
History of recent surgery, <30 days, Reduce value by 10%
History of CHF or COPD, Reduce value by 50%
ASA > II, Reduce by 100%

Fat Aspirate Maximum utilizes the formula:
Calculation: =(0.05*(patient height in meters))*(patient weight in kg)*(1-(-patient adjusted % total/100))

Anticoagulation Recommendations per Stephan and Kenkel and following Caprini Recommendations: PREVENTION AND TREATMENT OF VENOUS THROMBOEMBOLISM, International Consensus Statement, (Guidelines according to scientific evidence), Under the auspices of the Cardiovascular Disease Educational and Research Trust, Cyprus Cardiovascular Disease Educational and Research Trust, European Venous Forum, International Surgical Thrombosis Forum, International Union of Angiology and Union Internationale du Phlebologie.

Updates and Advances in LiposuctionPhillip J. Stephan, MD, FACS; and Jeffrey M. Kenkel, MD, FACSAesthetic Surgery Journal30(1) 83 -97(c) 2010 The American Society forAesthetic Plastic Surgery, Inc.

Fluid Restoration and Maintainence (based 4/2/1 anesthetic formula, first 10 Kg is 4 cc, second 10 Kg 2 cc and next Kg are 1 cc x hours npo, then that same amount ccs per hour of surgery), then using the formula of an additional 60 ccs per liter of fat aspirate over 5 liters.

Overnight Recommendations:
No references

Tumescent Formulations:
Hunsted formula
Lidocaine toxicity based on 35 mg/kg although some reports are higher

Individuals with poor skin tone, medical problems, obesity, or unrealistic expectations may not be candidates for liposuction.
